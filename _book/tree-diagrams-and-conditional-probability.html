1.8 Tree Diagrams and Conditional Probability

When calculating a conditional probability using a tree diagram, it helps to remember that the products of the probabilities along branches give intersections of events.

The denominator in the conditional probability formula, \(P(B)\) is the sum of all of the intersections in which event \(B\) is satisfied, whilst the numerator, \(P(A \cap B)\) is the single intersection which satisfies both events \(A\) and \(B\).
